Here’s a concise summary of the Kids’ Choice Awards “The Award, itself” article:

• Overview  
  – Annual Nickelodeon ceremony (since 1987) honoring kids’ favorites in TV, film, music and pop culture.  
  – Winners chosen by children’s votes; trophy is a hollow blimp (orange since 1990).  
  – First “ceremony” (1987) was four episodes of Rated K For Kids By Kids; voting by mail; trophy was a golden teleidoscope.  
  – From 1988 on, producers Alan Goodman, Albie Hecht and Fred Seibert revamped it into a live show with celebrity hosts and slime stunts.  
  – Has aired every year except 1993; spun off Kids’ Choice Sports in 2014 (merged back into the main show in 2020).

• Nomination Process  
  – Pre-nominations list eligible candidates for each category; main ceremony held the year after eligibility.  
  – Television categories may award blimps in colors other than orange.

• Special Awards  
  – Hall of Fame Award (1991–2000): gold blimp; kids voted from a multi-category slate.  
  – Wannabe Award (2001–2008): silver blimp; kids’ role model, chosen without voting.  
  – Only dual Hall of Fame and Wannabe winner: Will Smith.  
  – Big Green Help/Big Help Award (2009– ): honors environmental efforts; blimp was green initially, later silver.  
  – Lifetime Achievement Award (gold blimp; 2014 Dan Schneider; 2023 Optimus Prime—speech quoted).  
  – Generation Change Award (introduced 2019 in Sports; now in main show): for positive social impact.
